>
> Skills. People. Capital.
>

> 2/ **Skills.** Are you uniquely good at a core skill useful to others?
>
> Storytelling, pattern recognition, taste for important problems, teaching, building a community — find what feels like play, then let it consume you. You'll outcompete anyone treating it like a chore.

> 3/ **People.** Who trusts you, and how much?
>
> Breadth-first = following on a platform. Depth-first = a few deep relationships. Both compound. But for sanity, you need at least one person you can be vulnerable with.

> 4/ **Capital.** The universal religion. A stored form of leverage.
>
> It can be traded for almost anything — assets you can't build, skills you don't have, or bets on people you believe in. And unlike time, capital can be grown.

> 5/ Gradient descent on "what feels right" lands you in local maxima.
>
> Better: choose your North Star teleologically. What is your life _for_?
>
> Then reverse-engineer which skills, relationships, and capital you need to get there.

> 6/ The surprising part: optimal stopping theory (the secretary problem) says you should explore roles and skills until ~**age 29–30**, not 35.
>
> That's 1/e of adult life, adjusted for the fact that your 20s carry more subjective weight than your 50s.

> 7/ Joe Liemandt ran this playbook at scale.

## 3. $20 on X Ads Manager — step-by-step

**Do not use the X Ads API.** It requires separate approval beyond developer access and is designed for agencies running $10k+/mo. Use `ads.x.com` directly.

**Do not use Quick Promote.** It only targets by location/age/gender and has no keyword/follower-lookalike options. You'll pay to reach random people.

### Setup

1. Go to `ads.x.com` → switch to your `@vedant__khanna` ads account.
2. **Post the organic thread first.** Wait 6–12 hours for organic engagement data.
3. Pick the single tweet that got best organic engagement — that's the one you'll promote. Usually tweet 1 (hook) or the one with the stat.
4. Click **Create campaign** → choose objective.

### Campaign objective

Pick **Engagements** or **Website Traffic** (the tweet has a link, so Website Traffic is cleaner attribution).

- Do **not** pick *Reach* — with $20 the algo will just burn budget on cheap impressions to bots and dormant accounts.
- Do **not** pick *Awareness* — same reason.
- Do **not** pick *Followers* — you want readers, not follows.

### Budget

- Daily budget: **$10**
- Duration: **2 days**
- Bid: **Auto bid** (manual bidding at this budget creates underdelivery)

### Ad creative

Use the exact tweet from the thread — the algorithm favors promoted posts that already have organic engagement signal. Do not write a new "promoted-only" tweet for this.

### Kill criteria

After 24h, check the dashboard. Kill the campaign early if:

- CPM > $15 (you're paying for bots or over-competing for audience)
- Link CTR < 0.5%
- No engagement (likes/RTs) from verifiably real accounts in 12h — the targeting is wrong

If CPM < $8 and CTR > 1.5%, the campaign is working — consider adding another $10–20 after hour 24 rather than letting it burn out on day 2.

### Honest expectation

$20 at $6–10 CPM = ~2,000–3,000 impressions, ~30–100 link clicks, ~5–15 new followers. That is small. The point is not reach — it is seeding the algorithm with engaged signal so the organic tweet is pushed further, and testing which targeting bucket actually resonates for future, larger spends.
